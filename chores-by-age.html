Age Appropriate Chores for Kids

Use this guide to match chores and rewards to your child's current development stage so routines stay realistic, motivating, and consistent.

Quick fact: Age-appropriate chores range from sorting toys at age 5 to handling laundry and meal prep by age 14. Research links regular chore routines with stronger executive function, self-reliance, and family contribution habits. Small, repeatable tasks with clear rewards are easiest for kids to sustain long term.

Expert Consensus: Why Chores Help Development

Children build executive function when they complete routine multi-step tasks in the same order each day.

Consistent chores increase self-efficacy because kids can see direct progress from effort to outcome.

Family contribution language ("everyone helps") is more effective than punishment framing for long-term behavior.
